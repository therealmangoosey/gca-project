GLOBAL OVERVIEW

This archive collates publicly acknowledged continuity of government (COG) instruments, emergency powers, and public emergency response manuals relevant to large-scale biological threats and societal disruption. Entries list: document titles, owning agency, public access guidance, and a concise note on authorities such as martial law triggers and border closure mechanisms.

Reader's note

This resource intentionally omits operational-level granularity, exact coordinates, and procedural steps that could enable misuse. Use it for research, planning, historical reference, and academic analysis.

United States — Selected Dossiers

Publicly referenced documents and agency owners. Sensitive operational details omitted.
CONPLAN 8888-11
Owner: USSTRATCOM. Purpose: training CONPLAN; historically referenced as a non-standard training document. Public references available via USSTRATCOM/FAS archives.
Other Key Instruments: National Response Framework (FEMA), FEMA P-1000 (individual preparedness), Stafford Act.
Authorities: Insurrection Act (10 U.S.C. §§ 251–255); Quarantine laws (42 CFR Parts 70 & 71).
Agency owners: FEMA, DHS, CDC, USSTRATCOM.
Public access: FEMA.gov; CDC.gov; Congressional/GAO archives.

United Kingdom — Selected Dossiers

Civil Contingencies Act 2004
Owner: Cabinet Office; legal framework for emergency powers, local responder duties and temporary regulations.
Operation Temperer — military support to policing following specific threat thresholds (publicly described by UK government).
Agency owners: Cabinet Office, Home Office, UKHSA, MOD.
Public access: legislation.gov.uk; gov.uk.

Russia — Selected Dossiers

EMERCOM / Unified State System for Emergency Prevention and Response
Owner: EMERCOM (МЧС). Covers response to natural and man-made emergencies and civil defence coordination.
Notes: Closed administrative territories (ZATO) persist historically as restricted areas; national continuity doctrines referenced in Russian legal texts.
Public access: EMERCOM public materials and Russian legal portals; translated analysis available through academic/OSINT outlets.

China — Selected Dossiers

National Emergency Response Plan for Public Emergencies
Owner: State Council / Ministry of Emergency Management. Governance includes infectious disease and public health emergency response.
Agency owners: National Health Commission, Ministry of Emergency Management, PLA (for support roles).
Public access: State Council white papers and official directives (English translations available via WHO / academic sources).

Israel — Selected Dossiers

Home Front Command doctrine
Owner: IDF Home Front Command; ongoing state of emergency legal context in Knesset law.
Public access: IDF publications and Ministry of Health reporting.

Switzerland — Selected Dossiers

Federal Civil Protection Act
Owner: Federal Office for Civil Protection (FOCP). Switzerland maintains a distributed shelter system and federal coordination for emergencies.
Public access: admin.ch; FOCP documentation.

North Korea — Public & OSINT Dossiers

Public primary sources are limited. Use UN reporting, think-tank analysis (e.g., 38 North) and declassified intelligence summaries. Owner: DPRK leadership / Korean People’s Army.
Public access: UN reports, OSINT / satellite imagery analyses.

Germany

Japan

Sweden

Finland

Framework: Comprehensive security model combining civil defence and societal resilience.

Italy

Canada

Australia
